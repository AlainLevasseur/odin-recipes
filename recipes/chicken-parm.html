Chicken Parmesan

Description

Don't be fooled by the amount of steps! This recipe is simple and can be enjoyed
alone or be adapted for multiple servings (It makes for great left-overs!). The
thicker the cut of chicken the longer it takes to cook. This makes chicken
thighs a great substitute to reduce cook times.

Ingredients

  • Egg(s)
  • Panko
  • Chicken breast(s)
  • Scallion
  • Spaghetti
  • Mozzarella & Parmesan Cheese
  • Spaghetti Sauce

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 450 F (230 C)
  2. Season the chicken breast(s) with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Beat the egg(s) and place in a wide bowl. Use 1 egg per 2 chicken breasts.
  4. Lay out some panko on a small plate next to the egg bowl.
  5. Dip a chicken breast in the egg, coating it entirely. Let excess egg drip off.
  6. Place the egg covered chicken onto the panko and completely cover it with panko.
  7. Press lightly on the chicken to force the panko to stick to it better.
  8. Shake off gently any excess panko and transfer to a baking sheet.
  9. Pour a spoonfull of the spaghetti sauce on top of the battered chicken.
  10. Top the chicken with a sprinkle of Mozzarella.
  11. Repeat steps 5 through 10 for each piece of chicken.
  12. Boil the spaghetti to desired tenderness, while the chicken cooks.
  13. Slice thinly the scallion, separating the white top from the green stem.
  14. Mix the cooked spaghetti, white scallion top and spaghetti sauce.
  15. Plate the spaghetti mixture with a slice of cooked chicken on top.
  16. Sprinkle the plate with some parmesan cheese and the green scallion stem.
  17. Eat up!
